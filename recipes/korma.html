Vegetable and paneer Korma recipe

Description

This is an Vegetable and paneer Korma recipe. Indian/south asian cusine is one of my favourites as I find the meals comforting, not too unhealthy and I love the spice combinations. This Korma is full of vegetables and spices for nutrients and has paneer for a decent main protein source.

This recipe serves 4-5 people, the preparation time is 20 minutes and the total cooking time is 30-40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp cold pressed rapeseed oil
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 6 green cardomoms
  • 2 star anise
  • 3 inch cinnamon piece
  • 1 large/2 small bay leaves
  • ¼ tsp fennel seeds(optional)
  • 6 cloves
  • 6 medium cloves of garlic,finely chopped
  • 2 inch ginger,finely chopped
  • 1 large onion,finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes,chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 mild chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tin thick coconut milk
  • 3 tbsps plain low fat yoghurt/100mls soya cream(optional)
  • 2 medium carrots,chopped into cm cubes
  • small sweet potato,chopped into 3 cm cubes
  • 120g of green peas
  • small head of broccoli/cauliflour,chopped into small florets
  • 100g frozen spinach,defrosted
  • 450g paneer,cubed

    • Method

    1. Heat a pan with 2 tbsp of oil on a medium heat. When the pan becomes slightly hot,add whole spices.

      • 1 large bay leaf
      • 2 star anise
      • 3 inch cinnamon piece
      • 6 cloves
      • 6 cardomoms
      • ½tsp cumin seeds
      • ¼ tsp fennel seeds(optional)

    2. When the spices begin to sizzle,reduce the heat to low and add the ginger and garlic. Saute for a minute or 2 until they turn fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
    3. Add the chopped onions and ¼ teaspoon salt. On a medium heat, fry till onions turn golden adn transparent,stirring occasionally so the onions cook evenly.
    4. Add chopped tomatos,chilli and turmeric and saute for 2-3 minutes on a low to medium heat or until the tomatoes turn mushy. If they are too dry splash a little water on them.
    5. Add garam masala and coriander powder and saute for 2-3 minutes on a low heat without burning.
    6. Add the yoghurt and mix very well with the masala paste.
    7. Add the vegetables (except spinach), mix with the sauce and saute for 1-2 minutes.
    8. Add the coconut milk, mix well and bring to the boil (add a bit more water to adjust the consistency if required). Bring to a boil and reduce the heat.
    9. Add salt to taste then simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the vegetables are cooked and the sauce is a good consistency.
    10. Add the spinach 4 minutes before the sauce is done.
    11. Add paneer to the korma and turn off the stove. Give a good mix and keep the paneer korma covered.
    12. Add 2 to 3 tbsp chopped corander leaves(optional) and soya cream and give a stir.
    13. Serve up the korma with rice or naan.
