Vegetarian shephard's pie recipe

Description

This simple,hearty,vegetarian shephard's pie is packed full of healthy vegetables and lentils. Remember to use veggie/vegan worcestershire sauce,wine and parmesan (to make vegan you will also need to substitute the butter for a vegan one.)

This recipe serves 4 people, the preparation time is less than 30 minutes and the cooking time is 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Ingredients

For the filling

  • 1 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • 1 leek, trimmed,finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 150g chestnut mushrooms, roughly chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 chilli, deseeded, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp dried sage
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 400g tin green/Puy lentils, drained
  • 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 200ml vegetable stock
  • 200ml red wine
  • 1 tbsp worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • salt and pepper

For the topping

  • 2 sweet potatos (about 500g in total), peeled and cut into 2cm chunks
  • 2 floury potatos (about 500g in total),peeled and cut into 1.5 cm chunks
  • ½ small cauliflour (about 250g),leaves and root removed, separated into florets
  • knob of butter
  • 1 tbsp finely grated Parmesan(optional). Can use grated cheddar instead.
  • salt and pepper

    • Method

    1. Heat oil over medium heat and add the leeks. Fry for 4-5 minutes, or until beginning to soften.
    2. Add the carrots,mushrooms,garlic, chilli and herbs and continue to cook, stirring regularly, for 4-5 minutes.
    3. Add the lentils,tomatoes,stock and red wine and stir together until well combined. Bring the mixture to the boil, then reduce the heat until it is simmering and continue to simmer for 18-20 minutes while you make the topping.
    4. Preheat the oven to 200°C/180°C Fan/Gas mark 6.
    5. To make the topping, add the sweet potato and potato, to a large saucepan of boiling water and boil for 10-12 minutes (there's no need to add salt).
    6. Add the cauliflour and boil for a further 8-10 minutes, or until tender.
    7. Drain the vegetables well and return them to the pan. Add the butter,season well with salt and pepper, then mash until smooth. Set aside and keep warm.
    8. Stir the Worcestershire sauce (if using), soy sauce and balsamic vinegar into the filling mixture, which should have thickened during cooking. Continue to simmer for a further 1-2 minutes, adding a little water if the mixture is too dry (simmer for longer if the mixture is too watery). Season with salt and pepper.
    9. Transfer the filling mixture to an ovenproof dish, then spoon over the topping and spread into an even layer. If you like a crispy topping, create peaks in the mash using a fork. Sprinkle over the cheese, if using.
    10. Bake the shephard's pie in the oven for 18-20 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
    11. Serve the pie with salad or greens
